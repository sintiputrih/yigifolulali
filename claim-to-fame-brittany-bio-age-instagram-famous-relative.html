Meet Brittany, One Of The Contestants On Claim To Fame

When Brittany appeared on Claim To Fame, Logan, another contestant guessed that she was Terry Bradshaw’s daughter. Another contestant, Michael AKA Cubb Coleman guessed Brittany’s identity correctly on the day of the elimination. X AKA M. Lamar also figured out who her famous father was.

Speaking with Green Bay Press Gazette, Brittany shared about her relationship with her father. She wrote, “My dad and I are really close. I did inherit his competitive nature. At my house, winning is the only option”. She added, “I was born before he became successful so I got to witness the entire rise from an unknown to known. Don’t let my kindness fool you. I used to be an attorney and know how to win. I am so competitive that I hurt feelings”.

Brittany is one of the 12 contestants in season 1 of ABC’s show Claim To Fame. The show sees 12 celebrity relatives who try to find correctly discover celebrities they are related to. A guesser is voted/selected among the contestants and they will find the guessee to answer who the guessee is related to.

Kevin and Frankie Jonas, the oldest and youngest of the Jonas Brothers, respectively, host the program. The final person standing will get a prize of $100,000. All 12 candidates are connected to famous people and compete by trying to guess each other’s, famous relatives. Every week, a contender must guess the identity of another participant, and either they or the guessee are eliminated.

So, far two contestants are out.

The full name of Claim To Fame Brittany is Brittany Favre.

Who Is Claim To Fame Brittany Famous Relative?

Brittany Favre from Claim To Fame is the daughter of Green Bay Packers MLB legend Brett Favre. Brett was born on 10 October 1969 in Gulfport, Mississippi. He attended Hancock North Central High School where he discovered his passion for baseball and football.

Later, he attended the University of Southern Mississippi for college and graduated with a B.A. in Special Education.

In the second round of the 1991 NFL Draft, the Atlanta Falcons selected Brett Favre with the 33rd overall pick, however, he only played for them one season. Later, in 1992, he was sent to the Green Bay Packers, where he played quarterback for 16 years. He played for the Minnesota Vikings from 2009 to 2010, as well as the New York Jets from 2008 until his retirement.

Brett welcomed Brittany with his wife Deanne Tynes. They married on 14 July 1996 at St. Agnes Catholic Church in Green Bay, following his rehabilitation.

Brittany’s mother, Deanne is 53 years old being born on 29 December 1968 in Kiln, Mississippi, USA. After graduating in 1986, she obtained a basketball scholarship and enrolled in a community college in Poplarville, Mississippi.

She swiftly changed schools though, enrolling at the University of Southern Mississippi, where she graduated in December 1994 with a B.A. in exercise science.

Deanne was diagnosed with breast cancer in 2004. She underwent chemotherapy and radiation treatment for four months. She became cancer free in 2005. Her recovery inspired her to be her work as a breast cancer activist to help raise awareness.

The Deanna Favre Hope Foundation, where she currently serves as CEO, was founded when she started selling pink Green Bay Packers hats to collect money for charity.

Brittany also has a sister named Breleigh who was born on 13 July 1999.

During her 2015 interview with Bleacher Report, Brittany shared that the most important she learned from her dad was “that at the end of the day, you just have to put in the work. There’s really no way around it. Money can get you to a point, and fame can get you a little further, but no one is going to pay me if I can’t do my job well. Connections are a foot in the door, but I have to keep the door open”.

Claim To Fame Brittany Job

Claim To Fame Brittany Favre-Mallion graduated from Loyola University’s College of Law in 2015. Law school is not tough but she did it while raising two kids. Speaking with Bleacher Report she shared that she wanted to be a lawyer since she was a kid.

She said, “I looked at successful lawyers the same way aspiring athletes look at my dad. Naturally, I’ve changed paths a few times, but it was more to do with my level of self-confidence and less to do with my dream of becoming a lawyer. I had a little too much fun my first two years of college, so I didn’t think getting accepted to law school would be an option”.

Brittany has been working as a lawyer at Hattiesburg, MS. She worked at Green Law Firm PA. She was admitted to a Mississippi bar in 2015 and a Louisiana bar in 2017.

Brittany is also working as a part-time artist.
