Fabio Lanzoni Wiki Biography

Fabio Lanzoni was born on the 15th March 1959, in Milan, Italy of Italian ancestry. He is an actor and model, used many times depicting characters in various novels during the 80s and 90s. Fabio has been accumulating his net worth being active in modelling and show business since the 1980s.

How rich is the actor and fashion model? It has been estimated that the overall size of Fabio’s net worth is as much as $15 million. The sources of his wealth are films, modelling and television.

To begin with, Fabio was raised by his father, Saura Lanzoni, a manufacturer of belt conveyors, and his mother – the former beauty queen Flora Lanzoni. Fabio began his modeling career at the age of fourteen in Italy. After serving two years of military service, he moved to New York and signed with the Ford Modeling Agency. In 1987, he appeared on the cover of the romance novel “Hearts Aflame”. In 1988, he characterized the hero of the video game “Ironsword: Wizards & Warriors II”. In 1991, he abandoned New York, as like Sylvester Stallone and Arnold Schwarzenegger, he wanted to be the new hero of an action movie.

In 1992, hired by Robert Gottlieb from the William Morris Agency, Fabio topped the covers of more than two hundred and fifty books, video cassettes and DVDs including “Pirate”, “Champion”, “Mysterious” and many others. He appeared in the role of an angel in the horror film “The Exorcist III” (1990) with George C. Scott. He was also seen in the comedy directed by Paul Mazursky “Scenes from a Mall” (1991) with Bette Midler and Woody Allen. Fabio was in the main cast of the comedy fantasy film directed by Robert Zemeckis “Death Becomes Her” (1992). He has also appeared in the episode of the ABC sitcom “Roseanne” (1993) as security guard Lisle. The same year he showed up in the series “Acapulco HEAT” (1993-1994) as Claudio. Fabio then appeared in the ABC sitcom “Step by Step” (1997) with Patrick Duffy and Suzanne Somers, and later he starred in the comedy, fantasy sci-fi film “Dude, Where’s My Car ?” (2000) directed by Danny Leiner; Ben Stiller’s comedy “Zoolander” (2001) and the CBS soap opera “The Bold and the Beautiful” (2012). Overall, the characters Fabio has created have added sums to the total size of his net worth.
