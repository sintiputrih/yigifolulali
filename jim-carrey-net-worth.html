Early Life

Originally, James Eugene Carrey, Jim Carrey was born on January the 17th, 1962, in the suburb of Newmarket, Ontario, Canada. His father, Percy Joseph Carrey, was a musician and accountant and his mother, Kathleen Carrey, was a homemaker.

He grew up a Roman Catholic with his three older siblings John Carrey, Patricia Carrey, and Rita Carrey.

Percy Joseph Carrey was of French-Canadian ancestry and his mother, Kathleen Carrey, was of French, Irish and Scottish descent.

Jim Carrey attended Blessed Trinity Catholic Elementary School in North York.

He spent his early childhood days in Scarborough, Ontario, until his family moved to Burlington, Ontario. While in there, Jim went to Aldershot High School.

As time went by, the family went homeless and lived together in a Volkswagen van while teenage Jim and his brother spent living in a tent in Charles Daley Park on Lake Ontario.

The family struggled economically, but their situation started getting better after his father found a job as an accountant at the Titan Wheels tire factory.

Jim and his brother John worked as janitors and security guards at the tire factory in return for living in the house.

Jim started attending Agincourt Collegiate Institute after moving back to Scarborough. However, he dropped out of college on his sixteenth birthday.

He started performing comedy in downtown Toronto while still working in the factory.
