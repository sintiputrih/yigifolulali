Bert Kreischer Biography

His full name is Albert Charles Kreischer Jr., but everyone calls him “The Machine.” He’s an American stand-up comedian, podcaster, reality TV host, and actor.

In 1997, during his time at Florida State University, he gained fame. Rolling Stone magazine featured him. They gave him the title of “the ultimate partygoer” at the nation’s top party school. This inspired the 2002 film National Lampoon’s Van Wilder.

Kreischer has hosted various TV shows, including Hurt Bert on FX, Bert the Conqueror, and Trip Flip on the Travel Channel. He even starred in The Machine, a comedy film loosely based on his life.

Through his production company, Berty Boy Productions, Kreischer creates and hosts podcasts. Some of these podcasts include Bertcast, Open Tabs, and the cooking show Something’s Burning. Additionally, he co-hosts the podcast 2 Bears, 1 Cave alongside Tom Segura.

Early Life – Bert Kreischer Age

Kreischer was born on November 3, 1972, in St. Petersburg, Florida. Presently Bert Kreischer’s age is 51. He was raised in Tampa, Florida. He was born named Albert Charles Kreischer Jr.

Bert Kreischer Family

His father was Al, and he worked as a real estate attorney. His mother was Gege, and she was a teacher. Later on, she pursued a career in early childhood development. He has two sisters named Annie and Kottie.

Bert Kreischer Education

He attended a local elementary school. Later, he enrolled in a private Jesuit high school. After that, he went to Florida State University for higher education. At the university, he pursued an undergraduate degree in English.

During his college years, he gained notoriety for hosting extravagant parties. These parties led to him being named the top partier at the country’s Number One Party School. This recognition was bestowed upon him by Rolling Stone magazine.

His college experiences served as an inspiration. The inspiration was for the 2002 film “National Lampoon’s Van Wilder.” In the film, the titular character was portrayed by Ryan Reynolds.

Personal Life – Bert Kreischer Wife | Bert Kreischer Daughter

Bert Kreischer and LeeAnn Kemp got married in December 2003. They have two daughters. Their daughters’ names are Georgia (born in 2004) and Ila (born in 2006). The family resides in Los Angeles. LeeAnn hosts her podcast “Wife of the Party” from Bert’s man cave.

Bert Kreischer Career

Bert Kreischer Stand-up Comedy

Kreischer’s comedy journey began at Potbelly’s, a Tallahassee bar. He later moved to New York City after a talent agent discovered his stand-up tape. In New York, Kreischer worked at the now-closed Boston Comedy Club as a doorman.

Known for his shirtless stand-up performances, Kreischer is recognized for his storytelling skills. His storytelling prowess shines in his famous “The Machine” story.

It’s about an incident in Russia involving the Russian mafia during a college trip, and a movie adaptation was released in May 2023.

In 2004, Kreischer was featured on the DVD release National Lampoon Live: New Faces – Volume 2. During the same year, one of his stories appeared on Comedy Central’s animated series Shorties Watchin’ Shorties.

As a stand-up comedian, Kreischer has performed internationally. He has also made appearances on late-night talk shows. These shows include Late Show with David Letterman, Jimmy Kimmel Live, and Conan.

He was also a regular guest on Rachael Ray from 2011 to 2015. His first comedy special, “Bert Kreischer: Comfortably Dumb,” aired on Comedy Central in 2009.

In 2015, he returned to the network for the storytelling series “This Is Not Happening.” During the series, he recounted an episode involving wrestling a bear. Showtime released “Bert Kreischer: The Machine” in 2016.

Netflix has produced three of his comedy specials. These specials include “Bert Kreischer: Secret Time” in August 2018 (filmed in Philadelphia).

“Bert Kreischer: Hey Big Boy” was released in March 2020 (filmed in Cleveland). Lastly, “Bert Kreischer: Razzle Dazzle” premiered in March 2023 (filmed in Omaha).

Bert Kreischer Podcasting

Kreischer is the creator and host of the podcast Bertcast. He is also the creator and host of Open Tabs. Additionally, he hosts the cooking show Something’s Burning.

Bertcast launched in 2012. It used to be recorded from his specially built Man Cave. The Man Cave was featured on the home improvement reality show “Man Caves.”

Additionally, he co-hosts the podcast “2 Bears 1 Cave” with Tom Segura. Kreischer has made guest appearances on various podcasts.

These include WTF with Marc Maron and Doug Benson’s Doug Loves Movies. Also on the list are H3 Podcast, Your Mom’s House, and The Joe Rogan Experience.
