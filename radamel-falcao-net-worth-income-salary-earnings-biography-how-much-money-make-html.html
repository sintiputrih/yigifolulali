He is best known in South America by the names Radamel Falcao, Falcao Garca, and Falcao. He was born on February 10, 1986, in Bogota, Colombia, and is a Colombian football striker who now plays for Monaco and also represents his country’s national team in international competition. Known as El Tigre (Spanish for “The Tiger”) and the “King of the Europa League,” he is regarded as one of the best players in Europe. Falcao has been rated as one of the world’s best attackers by many publications. Falcao was the first Colombian to win the Golden Ball award, which he earned in July 2011 after winning the competition in Portugal. In the 2012 FIFA Ballon d’Or competition, he finished fifth. In addition, he finished as the tournament’s leading goal scorer on both occasions. As a result of winning his first UEFA Super Cup in 2012, Falcao became just the second player in history to do so, and the first since the competition’s reintroduction in 1998 to score a hat trick in the competition. Falcao scored five goals in one game against Deportivo La Corua on December 9, 2012, to lead his team to a 6–0 win at home.
