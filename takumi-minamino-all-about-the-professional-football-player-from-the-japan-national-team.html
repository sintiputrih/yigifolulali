Professional Journey

Takumi began playing football with the Cerezo Osaka youth squad when he was 12 years old. Red Bull Salzburg pursued him for more than a year before signing him on January 7, 2015. He was a member of this club for four years. He participated in several competitions and tournaments with the squad. He had an outstanding performance for the club. Takumi was signed by Liverpool in December 2019 after his $8.6 million release clause was activated.

He was the first Japanese player to play for the club. He was loaned to Southampton on February 1, 2021. He was a member of the squad for one Premier League season. Then, on November 20, 2021, he returned to Liverpool. He signed a four-year deal with AS Monaco on June 28, 2022. Takumi made his national team debut on October 13, 2015, in a friendly match against Iran. He was chosen for the Japanese national squad for the 2016 Summer Olympics 2016.

Will Takumi replace him at the FIFA World Cup in Qatar in 2022?

Japan’s national team qualified for the FIFA World Cup 2022 by finishing second in the AFC third-round Group B. Takumi Minamino was selected from the list of 26 players revealed by Japan’s national team management. As a result, he will compete in the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar. He will play midfield for Japan’s national team in the FIFA World Cup 2022.

His ambition was to play for a Premier League team.
