Is Taylor Swift & Travis Kelce's Relationship A PR Stunt?

In January 2024, Chiefs' owner Clark Hunt finally weighed in on the rumors that Kelce and Swift's romance was just a PR stunt. During an appearance on Mad Money on CNBC, Hunt said they were the "real deal" and that they were all "so happy" for the athlete. "Well, first of all, we're so happy for Travis Kelce and Taylor in their relationship. I've had a chance to meet her at one of the many games that she's attended this year," said the Chiefs' CEO.

He continued: "She's just as sweet as she can be. I also got to see her play here [at our Arrowhead Stadium] twice this past summer and what an incredible performer. I mean, just one of the most incredible artists of our time." So it's no wonder Swift "organically" increased their female audience.

"It's been a very interesting, very fun year having the two of them dating," Hunt explained. "The attention that's been focused on the Chiefs, our female audience has grown leaps and bounds. That's right, it was purely organic."

"You know, when they were just starting off dating, there were a lot of people saying, 'Oh, it's a marketing stunt,' but no, it's very real," Hunt said of Kelce and Swift's relationship.

Taylor Swift Might Have Gotten The "Ick" On Travis Kelce's Super Bowl Antics

Amid their PDA moments at the Super Bowl, fans noticed that Swift might have gotten the "ick" on Kelce after he chanted, "Viva Las Vegas" over and over during the trophy ceremony. In the video below, you can see the Lover songstress' expression change from proud to unsure. "Taylor swift definitely got the ick 3 seconds into travis kelce's speech," someone wrote on X (formerly Twitter).

Another one noted: "Bro no way Travis Kelce just gave Taylor Swift the ick live in tv in front of millions of viewers. I believe in the Ick now." Still, Kelce's fans came to his defense, saying he was just happy about their victory. "Taylor Swift fans saying she got the ick from Travis Kelce yelling 'Viva las vegas' over and over. Brother, If I won a superbowl I'd be up there with my shirt off and slapping my balls on the mic for all I care," said a netizen.

Dr. Louise Mahler, a body language expert, also weighed in on the issue, saying that the 14-time Grammy winner was "embarrassed" and was "being polite" throughout the viral footage.

"We can imagine how anyone super excited may have expressed themselves like this, but I think she was horrified, slightly amused, but embarrassed," the body language expert said of Swift's "ick" moment.

Mahler added: "When one sees Taylor Swift at the Grammys [you can tell] she is totally elated by her colleagues' wins. She jumps up and down and laughs and calls out. In this footage, she opens, then shuts and holds her mouth tightly. She does not want to open it, suggesting she does not want to say something. Her mouth is slightly smiling, but her eyes lose their sparkle. She is being polite."

The Kansas City Chiefs' Super Bowl win might have just proved that the "Taylor Swift Curse" isn't real.
